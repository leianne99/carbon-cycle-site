🌊 Ocean Absorption

The ocean is a massive carbon sink — it absorbs about 25–30% of the CO₂ that humans release into the atmosphere.

🌐 How It Works:

  • CO₂ in the air dissolves into the ocean’s surface
  • Some CO₂ reacts with water to form carbonic acid
  • Sea plants (like phytoplankton) use CO₂ for photosynthesis
  • Carbon can sink to the deep ocean and be stored for centuries

🐠 Why It's Important:

  • Helps regulate Earth's climate
  • Supports marine food chains and ecosystems
  • Stores carbon long-term in ocean sediments

⚠️ Problems to Watch Out For:

  • Ocean acidification: Too much CO₂ makes the ocean more acidic, harming corals and shellfish
  • Warming waters: Less CO₂ can be absorbed in warm water
